# Cheatsheet stub — Behavioral

> Generic, no personal data. Use with the `build-stories` skill, which turns *your* work into stories.

## STAR + Lesson (the structure)
- **Situation** — company, project, stakes. One sentence.
- **Task** — what was *yours*. Use "I", not "we".
- **Action** — three concrete things you decided/did.
- **Result** — quantified (%, $, time, scale), plus one memorable detail.
- **Lesson** — one generalizable sentence. This is the punchline; never skip it.

Target **90 seconds** spoken. Say each point once, then stop — repeating yourself reads as nervous.

## Evidence, not recital
Show the competency through a specific decision; don't assert it. "I'm a strong leader" (recital) →
"I set the technical direction and led 6 engineers through a 250× scale-up" (evidence).

## The meta-move (great for technical-behavioral)
**What you did → name the SOTA / standard approach → the trade-off you chose.** Signals you know the
textbook answer *and* why your real-world constraints led elsewhere.

## Common archetypes (prep one story each; stories should overlap coverage)
- Judgment under ambiguity · Disagreeing up / pushback · Failure with recovery · Leadership / direction
  · Conflict / collaboration · A risky bet & its trade-off.

## Pitfalls
- "We" instead of "I" (hides your contribution). · No number in the result. · A fake-failure ("I work
  too hard"). · Rambling past 90s. · Skipping the lesson.

## Done bar
Deliver each story in ≤90s, "I" not "we", with a real number and a one-line lesson — out loud, from memory.
