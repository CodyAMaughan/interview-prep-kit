## Day-by-day cadence
- **Day 1:** build-stories — draft all 5–7 STAR stories from the intake answers; flag the ⚠️ needs-a-number gaps (per-feature lifts, activated-invitees). [Stories now drafted in stories.md — refine + rehearse.]
- **Day 2:** research-prep — Kestrel company brief; nail the "why Kestrel" angle (experimentation-heavy growth = exact strength); draft HM playbook.
- **Day 3:** Homework — chase down the missing numbers (3 feature lifts, activated-invitees). Convert at least one ⚠️ to a real number; otherwise prepare to say "I owned the metric; I'd have to pull the exact lift."
- **Day 4:** build-curriculum — roadmap/backlog + PRD-craft track: read 1 strong PRD template, write a 1-page PRD for one of your 3 shipped Riverbend features retroactively. Learn RICE/ICE and apply it to a mock backlog.
- **Day 5:** build-curriculum — quarterly planning track: how a growth team sets a quarter (objective → bets → experiment portfolio). Be able to walk a quarter end to end.
- **Day 6:** Experimentation cheatsheet + a product-sense rep — sharpen MDE/power/CUPED/sequential/guardrails to instant recall; do one "design a growth feature for activation" case.
- **Day 7:** Rehearse all stories aloud to 90s; run ⭐ stories 1 and 3 through the `coach` subagent; tighten on feedback.
- **Day 8:** mock-interview — timed HM growth/experimentation case; grade with coach; fix the weakest dimension.
- **Day 9:** mock-interview — timed execution/roadmap case (this is the gap-aligned case); grade; fix.
- **Day 10 / Morning-of:** glance sheet (cheatsheets) — 90-sec pitch, 2 Kestrel facts, top 3 stories, 3 smart questions, namedrop lines (D7 22→28%, $1.5M LTV reallocation, −6% tutorial holdout).

## "Done" looks like
- Can defend every strong JD line cold: experimentation fundamentals, SQL/analytics, measurable funnel impact, ship/no-ship judgment — with the meta-move (what I did → SOTA → trade-off) for each.
- Can recall the gap topics cold: walk a growth roadmap + backlog prioritization (RICE) end to end, describe a full PRD's sections, and narrate how you'd run a quarter — even though you haven't owned these yet.
- Each of the 5–7 stories delivered in ≤90s, on a concrete decision + a real number (or an honest ⚠️ flag, never an invented one).
- Can name the gap out loud as a *credible stretch*: "experimentation half I own cold; roadmap/PRD/quarterly-planning half I'm growing into — and this role is weighted toward my strength."
- Can say "why Kestrel" in their own language: an experimentation-heavy growth PM role on a scaling consumer app is exactly where my experimentation depth + measurable funnel impact already live.
- Product-sense case no longer a cold spot: can run a "design a growth feature" prompt with structure, not just optimize an existing funnel.
